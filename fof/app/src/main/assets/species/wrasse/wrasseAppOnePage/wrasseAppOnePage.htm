In   general   they   are   colourful   fishes   the   sexes   having   different colouring or markings. Some    species,    probably    most    change    sex    with    age    from female to male and the colouration changes also.
Peak   feeding   is   during   August   in   the   daytime.   Quick   striking   is necessary to get the fish away from its crevice. The   swim   bladder   is   not   connected   to   the   gullet   so   the   fish   suffer if brought to the surface quickly. Float   fishing   will   allow   you   to   vary   depth   and   let   the   bait   sway   in the current to imitate the natural prey. Presentation is important as Wrasse have excellent eyesight.

NATURAL HABITAT

It   is   common   close   to   rocks   and   around   reefs   from   2   -   3   m   to about   20   m   while   the   young   are   often   found   in   shore   pools provided there is abundant algae and rock. Though   rarely   venturing   from   rock   crevices   and   sea   weed   or kelp   grounds,   they   can   be   found   on   their   own   or   in   small schools   using   their   pectoral   fins   in   a   rowing   motion   to   move about.

FACTS

A   large   family,   Labridae,   which   contains   some   600   species distributed in tropical, warm temperate, and temperate seas.
The   Ballan   Wrasse   is   the   largest   of   the   Wrasse   family   the smallest   member   is   the   Cuckoo   Wrasse   which   resembles   the fresh water Carp. All suffer in severe winters and can die from the cold. The   Wrasse   is   a   slow-growing   taking   ten   years   to   reach   1   and 20 kg to achieve a weight of 4.5. All   Ballan   wrasses   are   female   for   their   first   4   to   14   years before   a   few   change   into   males.   Large   Ballan   wrasses   are almost certainly male. After   six   years   the   female   matures   and   then   between   seven and   fourteen   it   changes   colour   and   sex,   continuing   to   live   to over   30   years.   She   spawns   in   June/July   after   constructing   a nest of algae in rock crevices. Wrasse   have   tough,   rubbery   lips   with   many   grooves   and   folds designed    for    tearing    limpets    and    other    shellfish    from    rock faces.    It    has    powerful    grinding    throat    teeth    and    strong flattened front teeth for eating molluscs and crustaceans. This   species   is   popular   as   a   food   fish   in   the   Orkney   Islands and   in   Galway,   although   it   is   not   highly   regarded   as   a   food fish in much of the UK and Ireland. In   recent   years,   it   has   become   a   popular   catch-and-release target     for     sport     fishermen     using     light     fishing     tackle, particularly those employing soft plastic lures. It   is   also   caught   commercially   and   kept   live   for   transportation to   fish   farms   to   clean   sea   lice   from   farmed   salmon.   However, the   ICFA   in   various   regions   especially   Devon   &   Severn   have taken   multiple   measures   to   manage   the   ‘Live   Wrasse   (pot) Fishery’   with   closed   seasons   whilst   spawning,   pot   limitations, minimum and maximum sizes and voluntary closed areas. This species can also be found in the aquarium trade.

Wrasse, Ballan  (Labrus bergylta)

There are restrictions in IFCA region Devon & Severn. Multiple   measures   are   in   place   to   manage   the   ‘Live   Wrasse (pot) Fishery’ in their area. Spawning     season     closure,     pot     limitation,     minimum     and maximum   sizes   and   voluntary   closed   areas   are   some   to      the restrictions. Check with local authorities before targeting this species.

DISTRIBUTION

Always   found   around   rocks.   Favourite   bait   can   be   peeler   crab, limpet or worms. After   striking   ensure   that   you   keep   the   rod   tip   high,   stopping the fish from diving into rock crevices. Use    a    long    shanked    hook    to    keep    you    fingers    away    from powerful       crushing       teeth       when       disgorging;       using forceps/disgorger   is   wiser.   Size   2   to   1/0   hooks   and   light   line maximum 15lbs is sufficient.
