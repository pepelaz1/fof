The   Pollack   is   a   popular   member   of   the   Cod   family   widely   distributed   in European waters; good for a tough fight when hooked. Like   Cod   they   are   plagued   by   parasites   and   use   grunting   noises   by vibrating   the   muscles   of   its   swim   bladder   when   mating   or   showing aggression. This   fish   resembles   the   Whiting   and   is   also   frequently   confused   with young Saithe though Pollack is brass coloured and much coarser. Pollack   are   benthic,   that   is,   they   live   near   the   sea   floor   and   considered to be relatively sedentary. Pollack    are    fast-growing    and    relatively    short-lived.    The    maximum reported age is 15 years. The   female   spawns   between   January   to   April   in   deep   water   at   100   - 200   m,   although   their   spawning   grounds   are   poorly   known;   a   study   of a   fjord   population   in   Norway   suggested   local   spawning.   The   eggs   and larvae   are   pelagic,   living   at   or   near   the   surface,   drifting   inshore   to hatch   and   mature.   Young   in   their   first   year   are   particularly   common   in shallow water. Pollack   is   an   important   species   in   recreational   fisheries.   Inshore   drift feathering,   trolling   or   spinning   at   dawn   and   dusk   or   on   dull   summer days produces the juvenile fish that live in shallow waters over rocks. Large   solitary   Pollack   in   deep   water   are   taken   by   using   a   Flying   Collar rig.   During   tide   runs   this   method   will   yield   good-sized   specimens, especially in mid-water above wrecks or reefs. Keep   the   bait   on   the   move   by   lowering   to   the   bottom   and   slowly retrieving.   Lengthen   the   trace   as   the   tide   slackens   so   that   there   is plenty of movement of the bait. On   bringing   the   Pollack   up   from   the   depths   the   swim   bladder   expands, so   do   this   slowly   to   avoid   impairing   its   resistance   or,   indeed,   killing   the fish.
POLLACK (pollachius pollachius)  aka: Lythe / Atlantic or European Pollack
