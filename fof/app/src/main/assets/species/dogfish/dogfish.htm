The   Dogfish   is   a   small   spotted   Catshark   that   is   often   difficult   to   avoid catching   when   fishing   along   some   parts   of   the   coast   with   small   or medium sized baits in waters of three metres or more. A   common   bottom-living   shark   which   occurs   in   depths   of   3   to   110   m and less commonly down to 400m. The   Dogfish   is   mostly   active   at   night,   roaming   between   the   sea   bed and   mid-water   in   search   of   food.   It   has   even   been   seen   'asleep'   during daytime,   but   due   to   its   numbers,   many   are   taken   in   the   daylight hours. Reproduction   continuous   throughout   the   year,   but   egg-laying   takes place   in   shallow   water   mainly   between   November   and   July.   They   are oviparous,   the   female   lays   seven   to   eight   eggs   in   pairs,   and   each enclosed   in   a   horny   transparent   yellow-brown   capsule   some   5   cm   long by   2-3   cm   wide,   often   called   a   mermaid's   purse,   which   have   a   springy filament   on   each   corner   that   attaches   to   sea   weed   and   other   objects on   the   sea   bed.   Incubation   lasts   for   5   to   11   months   then   hatch   at   a length   of   about   10   cm   and   sexual   maturity   is   attained   at   a   length   of about 50 cm. The   skin   of   this   species   is   highly   abrasive   and   before   sandpaper   came on   the   scene   had   been   used   as   a   substitute   for   pumice,   by   cabinet makers, French polishers and alabaster sculptors. Although   at   one   time   valueless   it   is   now   marketed   as   Huss,   Rock salmon,   Rock   eel   or   Sweet   William   and   forms   a   small   but   valuable fishery. In   other   parts   of   its   range   it   is   occasionally   baked   or   used   in   fish   soup but   the   fact   that   these   must   be   skinned   before   they   can   be   filleted discourages commercial fishermen from catching this species. Be   careful   when   handling   as   they   tend   to   curl   around   your   hand causing   abrasions   which   can   be   painful   and   without   treatment   become infected.
DOGFISH (scyliorhinus canicula)  aka: Doggy/Rough-hound/Flake/Rock Eel/Morgay/Catshark
