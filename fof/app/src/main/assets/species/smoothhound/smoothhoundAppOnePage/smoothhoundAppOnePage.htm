The   International   Union   for   Conservation   of   Nature   publishes a   European   Red   List   providing   a   review   of   the   conservation status   of   European   species   identifying   those   species   that   are threatened    with    extinction    at    the    regional    level,    so    that appropriate   conservation   action   can   be   taken   to   improve   their status. The   Smoothhound   conservation   status   is      ‘Vulnerable’   in   the Mediterranean     but     ‘Least     Concern’     In     Northern     Europe however   there   is   a   note   on   the   IUCN   website   advising   that this is due for review.
The    Inshore    Fishery    and    Conservation    Authorities    (IFCA) have   considerable   responsibility   for   managing   fish   stocks   out to   6   miles   from   the   shore.   While   the   IFCA's   still   have   to adhere    to    the    rules    laid    down    by    the    European    Union Common   Fisheries   Policy   they   are   able,   through   bylaws,   to set   minimum   landing   sizes   that   exceed   the   European   Union's and    apply    to    all    fish    retained    including    those    caught    by recreational anglers.

SMOOTHHOUND, STARRY  

(Mustelus asterias)

It   is   found   in   temperate   and   tropical   seas.   Large   schools   live off   the   south   and   south   west   coasts   in   shallow   waters   but   also as deep as 70 m most common on sand and gravel bottom. It   is   rather   uncommon   in   northern   European   waters   although it is found as far north as Denmark.
Additional    help    to    identify    this    species    can    be    found    in Google images using the following link;   www.google.com
Grey    above    and    on    the    upper    sides,    sprinkled    with    small white, star like, spots. Ventrally it is creamy white.
Not   normally   targeted   to   be   caught   and   when   done   so   it   is more likely to be returned to the sea unharmed. The   species   is   mainly   nocturnal   when   most   active   but   feeds incessantly   on   or   near   the   sea   bed.   Once   hooked,   the   fish takes off into tide so beware of fouling anchor lines. Treat   the   caught   fish   with   care   by   not   using   a   gaff   or   pulling out by the tail.
Grows   to   a   maximum   length   of   1.8   m   the   average   length   is near 1.2 m.
The   Starry   Smooth   hound   is   a   Hound   Shark   and   a   member   of the Trikidae family but not dangerous to man.
The   Starry   Smooth-hound,   a   member   of   the   shark   family,   and often   confused   with   small   young   Tope   or   the   Less   Common Smooth-hound that have the similar habitat and distribution. Its   teeth,   unlike   sharks,   are   short,   blunt   and   flattened:   well developed   for   crushing   the   shells   of   molluscs,   crustaceans, bivalves and sea urchins so is not dangerous to man. It   is   found   in   temperate   and   tropical   seas.   Large   schools   live off   the   south   and   south   west   coasts   in   shallow   waters   but   also as   deep   as   70m   most   common   on   sand   and   gravel   bottoms.   It is   rather   uncommon   in   northern   European   waters   although   it is found as far north as Denmark. Ovoviviparous;   the   embryos   develop   within   the   mother   where there    is    no    connection    with    the    maternal    membranes    and nourished    by    the    yolk    of    the    egg.    The    female    bears    from seven   to   fifteen   live   young   in   shallow   water   during   the   early summer,   each   measuring   up   to   30   cm   at   birth.   The   period   of gestation is approximately 12 months. In    the    Mediterranean    Sea    Mustelus    species    are    valued    for human    consumption    and    are    often    retained    and    marketed, whereas   in   northern   parts   of   the   Northeast   Atlantic   they   have little market value and are often discarded
