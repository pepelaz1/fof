The Black Bream is not black but has silvery grey sides with dusky bars. These colours fade soon after death when they turn almost black. Juveniles are more colourful and usually have numerous broken yellowish stripes along the sides, spotted fins and a dark edge to the tail which often persists into adult life. The Bream migrates from southern waters in April/June to spawn along chalk outcrops off the south and south west coast of England and is one of the few sea species in which the male builds a nest and then guards the eggs from predators until they hatch. There are several other varieties of Sea Bream around the British Isles, in particular red, spanish, couch's, bogue, gilthead and pandora. Almost all undergo a sex change during its life. Active mainly during the day, the best time to fish for Bream is before and after slack water as they prefer to feed during weak tidal runs. Once hooked, the Bream will give excellent sport on light tackle, especially if using fresh water spinning rod. Always use a net to land your catch as it may only be liphooked. Hold the belly when disgorging and beware of sharp dorsal spines. For bait use long strips of Squid or Mackerel (with or without a worm cocktail) which are most effective when presented above the lead weight.
