TACKLE:  LURES
What is a ‘Lure’? In   the   English   language   to   ‘lure’   is   to   tempt,   entice   or   allure   a person   or   animal   to   do   something   often   regarded   as   unwise especially by offering some form of reward. In   fishing   terms   this   means   using   something   usually   artificial (the   lure)   that   when   presented   correctly   will   attract   a   fish   to consider   it   as   a   tasty   meal   (temptation)   and   get   itself   hooked (unwisely). There   are   numerous   types   of   lure   available   that   can   be   made from   plastic,   wood,   feathers,   metal   or   rubber   some   of   which   are in   two   pieces   with   a   flexible   link   holding   them   together.   All   of which   have   unique   properties,   i.e.   colour,   shape,   number   of hooks   (single   or   trebles)   etc   which   when   used   in   a   certain   way   in the right waters will help catch the chosen species. You    can    never    tell    what    other    species,    other    than    the    one targeted,   may   be   tempted   by   any   of   these   types   of   lures   it   will just   be   by   chance   and   it   does   happen   which   comes   as   a   nice surprise. These are some of the types of lure available;
1.  Plugs: aka Crankbait, Popper or Wobbler. Plugs   are   hard-bodied   plastic   fish-shaped   fishing   lures.   Most normally   float   on   the   surface   so   that   after   casting   they   can   be retrieved   either   fast,   slowly   or   in   spurts.   Dependant   on   type they   react   in   different   ways   e.g.   the   design   of   Divers,   having   a vane   in   the   nose,   as   the   name   suggests,   will   descend   to   various levels. As   they   come   in   many   different   sizes,   shapes   and   colours   it would    be    impractical    to    provide    details    of    them    all.    Most, however, come under the following categories; • Plug: Short and deep-bodied shape • Minnow: Long and slender • Shallow-diver: Shallow diving capabilities • Deep-diver.
