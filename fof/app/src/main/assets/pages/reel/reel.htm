REELS

Designed   in   three   different   styles,   Fixed   spool,   Multiplier   and Centre   Pin   with   variations   within   each   group,   they   all   have   one common   denominator   and   that   is   to   store,   release   and   retrieve line.   Depending   on   the   type   of   fishing   will   attract   a   certain   style of reel. The   rod   it   has   to   be   fixed   to   will   also   determine   one   type   more suited   than   another;   essentially   the   rod   and   reel   have   to   be balanced. For   sea   fishing   from   a   boat   the   Multiplier   takes   preference   by many   anglers   but   there   are   many   Fixed   spool   converts   where-as fishing for coarse or game fish the Fixed spool are mainly used.
Centre   Pin:   The   simplest   of   reels   being   a   drum   with   no   gearing, not   suitable   for   casting   but   gives   direct   control   between   reel,   line and   fish.   Used   mainly   for   game   fishing   and   trotting   a   float   on rivers. This   is   the   only   type   of   reel   that   is   can   store   and   retrieve   wire line. Wire centre pin reels are usually quite large in diameter.
Spools These   should   always   be   filled   to   capacity   i.e.   within   l   mm   of   lip otherwise    the    bait    has    too    far    to    climb    to    peel    off,    thus restricting   casting   distance   and   making   every   cast   more   of   an effort than it need be. When   necessary,   use   old   line   or   wool,   etc,   to   back   up   spool before   filling   with   at   least   100   yards   of   line.   Closed   face   and centre-pin reels are happy with 50 yards of line.

Standard:    The   Standard   reel   has   a   skirt   on   the   reel   body   to prevent   line   being   blown   behind   the   spool.   It   was   never   very effective   and   is   almost   defunct   in   preference   for   the   Skirted Spool reel.
Skirted:   This   reel,   now   the   first   choice   amongst   fixed   spool reels,   has   a   skirt   on   the   spool   itself   and   almost   eliminates   the problem of line tangling behind the spool. The disadvantage is that spare spools are more expensive.
Closed   face:   The   baling   pin(s),   spool   and   line   are   all   enclosed behind   a   protective   casing   with   a   small   circular   entry   for   the   line to   be   accessed.   The   casing   prevents   many   of   the   problems   that wind   can   cause,   and   making   casting   a   one   handed   operation. This reel is best suited to light float fishing, specially on rivers.
