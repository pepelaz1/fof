We   cannot   all   catch   record   specimens   -   such   rare   moments   are   for the   lucky   few.   But   after   this   most   personal   of   contests   between fisherman   and   quarry,   the   satisfaction   of   landing   a   prize   specimen is   long   remembered   while   the   disappointment   in   losing   one   lasts only as long as it takes to bait up and try again. Indeed,   when   one   considers   all   the   variables   that   come   into   play: season,   bait,   tackle   let   alone   finding   a   hungry   fish,   perhaps   it   is surprising the numbers that are caught. Here   are   some   easy   to   follow   diagrams   and   illustrations   for   thirty two   common   fish   found   around   our   shores   together   with   some background notes and statistics on each. I   couldn't   resist   including   some   tips   on   preparation   and   cooking. Britain's   waters   abound   with   some   of   the   finest   fish   to   be   found anywhere   in   the   world   -   so   why   not   treat   your   family   and   friends to the fruits of your labours! There    are    few    experiences    to    beat    the    pleasure    of    fishing    in congenial   company   so   this   also   seems   an   appropriate   place   to record   special   thanks   to   my   friend   and   mentor,   Allan   Speake,   who provided   an   invaluable   wealth   of   information   and   the   tried   and tested practices of their part of the coast. Finally,   a   word   on   conservation.   Never   has   it   been   more   important to   maintain   the   delicate   balance   in   fish   stocks,   so   please   treat your   catch   with   care   and   try   to   put   back   unharmed   any   fish   that you   do   not   require   for   eating.   Each   one,   even   the   humble   Dogfish, has   its   role   to   play   in   the   ecological   balance   of   the   sea   and   in providing    for    the    survival    of    the    larger    and    more    interesting species. I   sincerely   hope   this   reference   on   fishing   brings   you   as   much enjoyment as it has to me during the research. Good luck and tight lines
