TACKLE: FLOATS
Floats Normally   associated   with   coarse   fishing   but   floats   have   a   place   in catching    certain    sea    species    such    as    Mullet,    Wrasse,    Garfish, Mackerel and even, as long as not protected, Sharks. This   is   great   sport   when   using   light   equipment   to   match   the   size of fish expected to be caught. Floats   are   manufactured   in   many   different   designs,   sizes,   and colours   and   each   type   has   a   specific   use.   Their   main   purpose   is to; 1. Aid casting 2. Indicate position of bail 3. Assist presentation of bait 4. Show bites. Sea   floats   are   large   in   comparison   to   coarse   floats   to   enable   them to   be   seen   more   easily   in   a   marine   environment.   They   can   be   tall or   short,   round   or   pear   shaped.   Attachment   to   the   main   line   is usually   made   by   passing   the   line   through   the   middle   and   using stoppers either at just the top or top and bottom. The best one to use will depend on the species and weather.
