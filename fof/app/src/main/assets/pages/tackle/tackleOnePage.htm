TACKLE & RIGS
Tackle According   to   Wickipedia   ‘fishing   tackle’   refers   to   the   equipment used   by   fishermen   when   fishing   and   the   term   ‘tackle’,   with   the meaning "apparatus for fishing", has been in use from 1398 AD!! Fishing    tackle    is    also    called    fishing    gear.    However    the    term fishing   gear   is   more   usually   used   in   the   context   of   commercial fishing, whereas fishing tackle is for recreational fishing. Almost   any   equipment   or   gear   used   for   fishing   can   be   called fishing   tackle.   Some   examples   are   hooks,   lines,   sinkers,   floats, rods,   reels,   baits,   lures,   spears,   nets,   gaffs,   traps,   waders   and tackle boxes. Gear    that    is    attached    to    the    end    of    a    fishing    line    is    called terminal   tackle.   This   includes   hooks,   leaders,   swivels,   sinkers, floats,    split    rings    and    wire,    snaps,    beads,    spoons,    blades, spinners   and   clevises   (like   shackles)   to   attach   spinner   blades onto fishing lures. Rigs The   term   rig   is   used   for   a   completed   assembly   of   tackle   made   up and ready for fishing. The   type   of   rig   selected   to   use   is   most   important   when   targeting certain species as they differ in the way bait is presented. There   are   many   types   of   rigs   with   numerous   variations   details   of some to the types including; • Booms • Feathers • Fixed • Float • Ledger • Match • Paternoster For   each   type   of   rig   there   are   certain   techniques   in   where,   when and how to use them and these can include; • Spinning • Trolling • Wrecking More   details   regarding   the   rigs   and   methods   can   be   seen   using this link.
